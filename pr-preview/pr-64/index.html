    👋 About me

    I am an undergraduate student and research assistant at Wuhan Polytechnic University under the supervision of Prof. Xiantao Qin. Since February 2024, I have been conducting independent research in Prof. Qin’s group. Within the field of civil engineering, my work centers on engineering materials at the interface of materials engineering and chemical engineering.

    My previous research has centered on key topics in low-carbon cementitious composites, hydration chemistry, CO2 mineralization, waste valorisation, microstructure–property relationships.

    In the future, I hope to pursue research across science and engineering to develop new approaches that address real-world challenges and contribute to a more sustainable future.

    🔬 Research Experience

    Prof. Xiantao Qin's Group, Wuhan Polytechnic University

    Wuhan, China

    Research Assistant

    February 2024 – Present
    • Modifying solid waste precursors through thermal treatment: Calcined phosphogypsum at 150-800°C in a muffle furnace to remove P/F impurities and transform gypsum phases, and measured whiteness, density, pH, and particle-size distribution under varied temperatures.
    • Tailoring alkali-activated solid waste cementitious composites: Formulated solid-waste-based mix designs for high-performance alkali-activated cementitious composites, integrated XRD, TG-DTG, and FTIR analyses to characterize hydration-product evolution and phase assemblage, identified a steel-slag-assisted microcrack sealing mechanism, and benchmarked binder sustainability against OPC and CSA cement using CO2 emission, energy, and cost metrics.
    • Carbonating solid waste precursors for CO2 mineralization and sequestration: Developed an ambient-condition aqueous carbonation method for efficient CO2 mineralization of steel slag, quantified phase assemblage, CO2 uptake, and particle size using QXRD Rietveld, TG, and laser diffraction, characterized carbonation product nucleation and growth using FTIR and SEM-EDS evidence, and established how carbonated steel slag enhanced PG-GGBS binder strength and water resistance.
    • Enhancing water resistance of solid waste binders through chemical modification: Tuned solid waste binder gel chemistry using NaAlO2 as a dual Al-Na source, quantified gel type proportions and transformation pathways through 29Si NMR spectral deconvolution analysis, and proposed and validated a gel transformation coefficient for evaluating water resistance in solid waste binders.
    • Modeling long-term degradation of solid waste concrete under water exposure: Designed an accelerated sulfate leaching protocol to simulate water resistance degradation under wet-dry cycling, built a COMSOL transport model to project sulfate migration and leaching-depth evolution over 360 cycles, and validated the model using 90 accelerated sulfate leaching cycles correlated with strength loss.
